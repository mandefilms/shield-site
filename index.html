How It Works

Set it up once. Then forget about it.

Shield is designed to run in the background of your family's life — not in the foreground of your anxiety.

1
🫥

Hide it

Tuck Shield into your child's bag, jacket lining, or a favourite accessory. It's small, light, and designed to stay put. Your child doesn't need to know it's there — or can be told it's their guardian owl.

2
📍

Set your safe zones

In the Shield app, draw zones around school, home, a grandparent's house — anywhere your child should be. Shield watches the boundaries for you.

3
☀️

Live your life

You don't need to stare at a map. Shield runs quietly, checks in silently, and stays out of your way. Check in when you want to — or just let it watch.

4
🔔

Get alerted only when it matters

If something's off — your child left a zone, didn't arrive at school, or pressed the SOS button — you'll know instantly. With location. With context. When you need it.

Child-Initiated Alert

A button for the moments your child needs you.

Sometimes kids just need a pickup. Sometimes it's more serious. Either way, one press sends an instant alert to a parent's phone — with location, time, and a clear signal that something needs attention.

  • Live GPS location shared immediately
  • Works silently — no need to speak or be seen

Meet the Companion

A little figure that keeps Shield charged.

Every Shield comes with a small printed companion figurine — a physical version of your child's mascot. It connects to the device via Bluetooth and lives on a shelf, a desk, or wherever your child loves most.

Press the heart on its chest and it tells you the battery level. That's it. Simple enough for a four-year-old, fun enough that they actually do it.

It turns "keep your Shield charged" from a parent's nagging into something a child wants to do themselves.

Our Story

Built by a family, for families.

👫
Mariessa and Evan
Auntie Reesa and Uncle Evan — Founders of Shield

Mariessa and Evan are a husband and wife filmmaking team who've spent years working in remote locations across the world. Through that work, they've seen how quickly things can go wrong — silently, suddenly, and in ways designed to go unnoticed.

"We wanted to give our four nieces an extra layer of protection. Not to watch their every move, but to alert someone the moment something seemed out of the ordinary."

Nothing on the market did that. The trackers they found were either too visible, too unreliable, or too dependent on a parent glued to a phone. So they built Shield themselves.

Shield isn't a tech company chasing a market. It's a family that solved their own problem — and decided to share the solution.
