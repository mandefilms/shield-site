The Problem

Existing trackers weren't built for real family life.

Most child safety devices solve a narrow problem — and create new ones along the way.

💸

Subscription fatigue

Most devices require ongoing monthly fees just to stay connected. Costs add up, coverage lapses, and families give up.

🎒

Kids lose them

Wearable trackers get left on desks, forgotten in bags, or simply taken off. If it's not on your child, it's not protecting them.

📱

Anxiety, not peace

Staring at a live map doesn't make you feel safer. It just means you're glued to your phone while life happens around you.

📡

Unreliable coverage

Devices that depend on Wi-Fi or Bluetooth fail the moment your child steps outside a familiar area.

How Shield Is Different

Built for the moments you can't predict.

Shield was designed around one idea: parents shouldn't have to think about it until they need to.

🫥

Hidden by design

Shield is concealed inside clothing, a school bag, or accessories. Children don't wear it visibly — so they can't lose it, forget it, or have it taken from them. Non-metallic construction keeps signal strong and provides body separation for RF safety.

🔔

Early alert, not just tracking

Yes, you can check the map whenever you want. But Shield's real value is what happens when you're not looking. It monitors silently and alerts you the moment something seems off — a missed arrival, a zone exit, an SOS press.

✅

One purchase. No fees ever.

Cellular data is included for the life of the device. No monthly subscription. No SIM cards to manage. No coverage that quietly lapses. You pay once, and Shield stays connected.

🌍

Truly international

Cellular coverage across 135+ countries. Built from the start for families who travel, move, or have children studying or living abroad.

Our Story

Built by a family, for families.

👫
Mariessa and Evan
Auntie Reesa and Uncle Evan — Founders of Shield

Mariessa and Evan are a husband and wife filmmaking team who've spent years working in remote locations across the world. Through that work, they've seen how quickly things can go wrong — silently, suddenly, and in ways designed to go unnoticed.

"We wanted to give our four nieces an extra layer of protection. Not to watch their every move, but to alert someone the moment something seemed out of the ordinary."

Nothing on the market did that. The trackers they found were either too visible, too unreliable, or too dependent on a parent glued to a phone. So they built Shield themselves.

Shield isn't a tech company chasing a market. It's a family that solved their own problem — and decided to share the solution.
