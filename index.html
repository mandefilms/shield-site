The Device

Small enough to disappear. Built to stay put.

Shield is designed to attach invisibly to clothing, the same way a security tag attaches to a garment.

A built-in motion sensor detects on-person movement, so we can tell it's actually being worn, not just left behind in a bag.

Three layers of encryption protect every signal Shield sends, and there's no Bluetooth beacon broadcasting its presence, so it stays undetectable to anyone scanning nearby.

How It Works

Shield is designed to run in the background of your family's life: not in the foreground of your anxiety.

1

Attach it

Shield is designed to be attached to your child's clothing or worn discreetly.

2

Set your safe zones and preferences

Draw zones around school, home, or anywhere your child should be. Safe zones are just one part of how Shield works. You can also set how often you receive location updates with the "out and about" feature, giving you the right amount of information without being overwhelmed. Shield will also learn your family's daily patterns over time and alert you only when something genuinely seems off.

3

Live your life

You don't need to stare at a map. Shield runs quietly, checks in silently, and stays out of your way. Check in when you want to, or just let it watch.

4

Get alerted only when it matters

If something's off, whether your child left a zone, didn't arrive at school, or pressed the SOS button, you'll know instantly. With location, time and device battery life.

SOS press to alert
Child-Initiated Alert

A button for the moments your child needs you.

Sometimes kids just need a pickup. Sometimes it's more serious. The button is simple: one press for an alert, two presses for a pick me up request. An instant notification goes to the parent's phone with location, time and device battery life.

  • One press sends an alert
  • Two presses sends a pick me up request
  • Works silently, no need to speak or be seen

Meet the Companion

A little figure that keeps Shield charged.

Each device comes with an optional add-on figure, a physical version of your child's companion. It connects to the device via Bluetooth and lives on a shelf, a keyring, or wherever your child loves most.

Press the heart on its chest and it tells you the battery level. That's it. Simple enough for a four-year-old, fun enough that they actually do it.

It turns "keep your Shield charged" from a parent's nagging into something a child wants to do themselves.

Our Story

Built by a family, for families.

Mariessa and Evan
Auntie Reesa and Uncle Evan, Founders of Shield

Mariessa and Evan are a husband and wife filmmaking team who've spent years working in remote locations across the world. Through that work, they've seen how quickly things can go wrong. Silently, suddenly, and in ways designed to go unnoticed.

"We wanted to give our family an extra layer of protection. To alert someone the moment something seemed out of the ordinary."

Nothing on the market did that. The trackers they found were either too visible, too unreliable, or too dependent on a parent glued to a phone. So they built Shield themselves.

Shield isn't a tech company chasing a market. It's a family that solved their own problem and decided to share the solution.
